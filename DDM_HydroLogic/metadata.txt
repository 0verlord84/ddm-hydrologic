about=Setting up a hydrologic model usually means days of GIS work before the
    modelling even starts: deriving flow paths, cutting subcatchments, measuring
    areas, then retyping it all into the model. DDM HydroLogic squeezes that
    into one interactive session in QGIS.

    Point it at a DEM and it builds a connected D8 flow-path network, drawn by
    Strahler order so the drainage reads at a glance. Click any reach to light
    up everything that drains to it, sketch a mask to keep the analysis where
    you want it, draw an outlet line, set a minimum subcatchment size, and the
    plugin cuts the catchment into clean, dissolved subcatchment polygons.

    Once the delineation looks right, the model files are one click each: a
    GeoPackage of flow paths and subcatchments, a RORB .catg control file, a
    WBNM .wbn runfile, an XP-RAFTS .xpx exchange file, or the seven TUFLOW
    region shapefiles. Topology, areas and coordinates come straight from the
    GIS; rainfall, losses and materials stay as clearly marked defaults, so the
    modelling decisions remain yours.

    Requirements and notes: QGIS 4.0 or newer, with no extra Python packages to
    install (it relies on the numpy and GDAL libraries already bundled with
    QGIS). Work in a projected CRS for meaningful areas and lengths. The model
    exports are first-pass scaffolds and need their own software to open and
    finish: RORB, WBNM, XP-RAFTS or TUFLOW.
